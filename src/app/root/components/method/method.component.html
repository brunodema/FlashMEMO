The Leitner Box

The Leitner System, also referred sometimes as 'Leitner Box', is a spaced-repetition memorization technique proposed by German science journalist Sebastian Leitner in the 1970s. In his book 'So lernt man lernen' (roughly 'how to learn to learn' or 'learning how to learn'), the idea of a review system is proposed, where concepts - which can be words, topics, names, etc - can efficiently be learned by reviewing them in pre-determined time ranges, which get longer and more challenging as you prove to correctly remember it. You can check more on the Leitner System here.

Fluent Forever and Flashcards

The use of flashcards is popularly used in conjunction with this method. Published by Gabriel Wyner, the book 'Fluent Forever' describes the author's experience with this method during the language learning process, and tips on how to take the most of it. As someone who read the book and sucessfully put the concept to the test, I decided to set up a platform on which users can easily create their own flashcards and help each other learn more efficiently, with the help of a collaborative environment. You can either check the book or go to Fluent Forever's blog to get more info about the team's idea, its app in development and its methodology.

(Language) Learning

As mentioned, the Leitner System isn't a 'language learning' methodology, per se. You can apply it to whatever you want, in fact. Are you having a hard time fixating concepts of a given class in college? Or do you wish to establish a more grounded study routine for an upcoming test? Maybe just train memorization for the sake of it - you might be participating in memorization contests, for instance? The goal of this app is not only to provide a flexible and intuitive system to create and store your flashcards, but also to leave up to you how to handle them. If you specify a target language for your deck, you'll be redirected to guided content for the given target language. If you don't, you'll be able to add whatever content you want to your deck/flashcard.
